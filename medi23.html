MEDI2023:

  • Abdelhakim Baouya and Brahim Hamid Formalizing Clock Deviation Detection and Treatment for Component-based Architecture. In 12th International Conference on Model and Data Engineering, 2023 (Submitted).

    Abstract: Modern cyber-physical systems, with their reliance on diverse computation logics, communication protocols, and technologies, are susceptible to environmental phenomena that can affect system behavior. Ensuring resilience in these systems requires considering such environmental factors during high-level design stages to enable accurate forecasting. In this paper, our approach focuses on modeling and analyzing the impact of clock deviations within a component–port–connector architecture model using a message-passing communication paradigm. To achieve this, we employ an architectural meta-model that leverages probabilistic automata to define the behavior of system components and connectors precisely. This is then interpreted through PRISM language for analysis purposes. Functional and non-functional requirements are expressed using probabilistic computation tree logic (PCTL). To validate our findings, we apply our approach to examine how clock deviations influence the orchestration of cranes via a drone system as a representative application scenario.
